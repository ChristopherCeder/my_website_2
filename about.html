About

the ceiling of their own business.

Photo of
Christopher Ceder

The kind of partner you didn't know you could hire

I've spent my career inside operations — not observing them from a boardroom, but working alongside teams to figure out why things weren't working and building the structures that made them work consistently.

I've seen what happens when a growing business outpaces its own systems. Good people making decisions without context. Communication that happens by accident, not design. An owner who started the business to pursue something meaningful, now stuck managing every detail of it.

That's the gap I fill.

As a fractional CEO and operations consultant, I work with a small number of owner-led businesses at a time — bringing the same strategic and operational focus you'd get from a seasoned executive, without the full-time cost or the distraction of another hire.

My background spans business intelligence, process design, leadership development, and organizational systems. I'm comfortable in the data, in the strategy room, and in the day-to-day operational conversations that actually move things forward.

How I Think

A few things that shape how I work

Clarity before solutions

Most business problems aren't what they appear to be on the surface. I spend time understanding the real constraint before proposing anything to fix it.

Honest over comfortable

I'll tell you what I actually think — including when the answer is difficult. Consultants who only tell you what you want to hear aren't helping you grow.

Systems over heroics

Individual effort is fragile. I'm not here to be the smartest person in the room; I'm here to build things that work without me in the room.

Fewer clients, deeper work

I keep a small client roster on purpose. When I'm in, I'm genuinely in — invested in your outcomes, not just logging hours.
